Research Proposal Summary
Title: Evaluating the DLS Method and Machine Learning Approaches for
Target Prediction in Interrupted Cricket Matches Using Explainable AI
1. Introduction
Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams, typically in formats defined by a
fixed number of overs per side. An “over” consists of six legal deliveries (“balls”). In limited-
overs cricket (for example, One Day Internationals – ODIs, or Twenty20 matches), each team
has a fixed number of overs in which to score as many runs as possible. When matches are
interrupted—commonly by rain or other external conditions—the game administrators must
adjust the target score (for the team batting second) in a way that is fair to both teams, given
the reduced number of overs or changed conditions.
The Duckworth-Lewis-Stern (DLS) method is the standard approach adopted by the In-
ternational Cricket Council (ICC) and other cricket boards for revising target scores in rain-
interrupted matches. It computes revised targets based on “resources” available to the teams
— that is, overs remaining and wickets in hand — rather than simply applying a proportionate
reduction in overs. The full methodology of the DLS system is described in the official ECB
Duckworth-Lewis-Stern Methodology Document.
While DLS has been widely accepted as a fair method, it is inherently rule-based and built
on assumptions of average scoring patterns that may not incorporate all modern dynamics (such
as changes in run-rates, evolving batting and bowling strategies, or T20 influences). In contrast,
Machine Learning (ML) approaches offer the possibility to build predictive models based on
large historical match-level data, capturing more complex interactions (such as pitch conditions,
team momentum, or players’ recent form) and adapting over time. Furthermore, with the advent
of Explainable AI (xAI), it is now possible to interpret ML model outputs to identify which
variables most strongly influence predictions, thereby adding transparency to model outcomes.
This research therefore aims to perform a comparative analysis of the DLS method versus
ML-based predictive models for interrupted matches, and to apply xAI techniques to interpret
which match variables drive the predictions. By doing so, it will assess whether ML-based
methods can complement or even outperform the DLS method in terms of fairness and accu-
racy, while providing interpretable insights for match officials, analysts, and cricket governing
bodies.
2. Research Objectives
The primary objectives of this research are:
1. To critically analyse the theoretical and statistical foundations of the DLS method.
1
2. To develop and train ML models (e.g., Gradient Boosting, Random Forests, Neural Net-
works) for predicting revised targets and match outcomes under interrupted conditions.
3. To compare the predictive performance of ML models against DLS using historical men’s
One Day International (ODI) match data.
4. To experimentally extend the analysis to women’s ODI and T20 matches to assess the
generalisability of both DLS and ML-based approaches across formats and genders.
5. To employ Explainable AI (xAI) methods—such as SHAP (SHapley Additive exPla-
nations) and LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations)—to identify and
quantify key variables (e.g., run-rate, wickets lost, overs remaining, pitch conditions)
influencing target predictions.
3. Methodology
This study will utilise historical men’s ODI match datasets as the primary data source, obtained
from publicly available cricket databases such as ESPN Cricinfo and ICC archives. Additional
experiments will be conducted on women’s ODI and T20 international match data to evaluate
the adaptability of ML models and the robustness of the DLS method across different formats.
Data preprocessing will involve normalization, handling of missing values, and feature engi-
neering (e.g., context-aware variables such as momentum and pressure indices). ML models
will be trained using cross-validation and evaluated using performance metrics such as RMSE,
MAE, and R2. The interpretability of model predictions will be analysed using xAI metrics
to ensure transparency and fairness. Comparative analysis will determine whether ML-based
approaches can provide more accurate and equitable target adjustments than DLS.
4. Expected Outcomes
The research aims to demonstrate the potential of ML-based frameworks to enhance the fairness
and accuracy of target predictions in interrupted matches. The inclusion of women’s ODI and
T20 formats will offer broader insights into how model behaviour and variable importance
differ across contexts. Additionally, the integration of xAI methods will provide transparency
regarding the most influential match factors, supporting a data-driven alternative to the DLS
method.
5. Keywords: Cricket, DLS Method, Machine Learning, Explainable AI, SHAP, Target Pre-
diction, Sports Analytics, ODI, T20.
